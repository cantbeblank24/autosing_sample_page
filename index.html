Abstract

In this work, we introduce AutoSing, a novel framework designed to generate high-quality singing voices from provided lyrics and musical accompaniment. Leveraging a text-to-semantic model to interpret lyrical content alongside the latent representation of the accompaniment, AutoSing leverages an autoregressive model to generate 32kHz vocal stems, achieving an output that is textually accurate and well aligned with melodical nuances of the musical accompaniment. Our contributions include a novel training scheme for autoregressive audio models applied to singing voice synthesis, various ablations on singer embeddings and. We measure AutoSing's performance on various objective metrics and perform subjective listening tests, demonstrating the capability of our approach in generating coherent and harmonious singing voices. Furthermore, we open-source our codebase to foster further research in the field.
